About the Project

PennyWise is a simple and user-friendly money-saving app. It helps users track income and expenses, set savings goals, and get personalized financial advice.

Design Thinking

I highlight the importance of design thinking, focusing on empathy, ideation, prototyping, and iteration. This approach helps me create innovative, user-centered solutions.

Problem Statement

Saving money can be challenging, particularly when time is limited. Users need a tool that offers personalized financial advice based on their actual finances to help them save effectively within a short timeframe.

Back to Table Of Contents

Enhance Solution

This app solves this problem by giving users personalized financial advice based on their actual income and expenses. The app helps users track their money, set savings goals, and offers tips to save money quickly, making saving easier even with a tight deadline.

Empathy Mapping

I created an empathy map to understand users' feelings, needs, and pain points, ensuring the app meets their financial management needs effectively.

User Personas

Through interviews with individuals using money-saving apps, I gained insights into their feature preferences and requirements. This information serves as valuable guidance for planning and designing my app to effectively meet their specific needs.

Style Guide

Color

I chose this color palette to create a user-friendly and professional interface. Bright blue conveys trust and reliability, while bright red highlights important alerts and notices. Dark blue-grey ensures text readability and balances the overall look. Teal symbolizes financial health, and gold represents success and motivation.

Image Style

Images will showcase money, savings, and financial health. Small illustrations may be used to reward users for reaching financial milestones.

Writing Style

  • Friendly
  • Reliable
  • Simple

Our voice is friendly and approachable, aiming to make users feel comfortable and supported. It conveys reliability and trustworthiness, ensuring users feel confident about using the app. The tone is professional yet simple. We emphasize empowerment and encouragement, helping users understand that saving money is achievable for everyone, regardless of their financial expertise.

Logo & Iconography

I chose to use a stylized penny or coin symbol for the logo because it serves as a powerful representation of savings and financial management. The familiarity of a coin immediately connects with users, conveying the app's purpose of helping them save money effectively.

Usability Testing

By testing prototypes with users, I find out some usability issues.

These were some major usability issues I need to focus on :

  • Additional ways to login/signup were missing.

  • A notification icon was needed.

  • Highlight the icon to let users know which page they are on.

  • There should be an option to edit the goal.

I then implemented these changes in my design.
